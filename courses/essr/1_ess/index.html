What is the ESS?

May 5, 2019

“He who leans against a good tree finds good shelter” - Sancho

The European Social Survey (ESS) is a social research project organised and administered by a network of academic institutions. It’s main activity is a cross-national survey conducted every two years since 2002, following the highest standards in social survey research.

ESS website

The ESS website is the best source for all infomation about the survey. There, you will find the questionnaires, technical documentation for each round and country, publications, an online analysis tool, as well as the data itself.

ESS material

Here we describe part of the survey’s attributes that you need to know in order to analyse the ESS data. This description is far from complete and if you want a comprehensive and better guide to analyse the ESS data, you shoul take a look at:

“Let papers speak and lips be silent” - Sancho

Countries

“In each country its custom” - Sancho

More than 20 countries participate in every round of the European Social Survey.

The ESS data is based on representative samples of the national resident population aged 15 or older. Each country is responsible for the translation of the questionnaire, the sample design and the data collection.

In practical terms, this means that one must pay attention to some national particularities when analysing the data. Some questions are nationally adapted (political parties, educational levels, religious denominations), some may have a high proportion of missing values, and the data collection periods may differ slightly.

Country-specific questions

In addition to the core questions administered in all countries, additional questions may have been added to national questionnaires. This country-specific data is not included in the final dataset and you will have to download them from a different area of the ESS website.

Rounds

“All times are not alike” - Sancho

One of the main objectives of the ESS is to capture social change and stability in Europe. For that, data collection is administered every two years during the final quarter of the year of reference. There have been eight rounds of the ESS to date (2002-2016).
